Meet

Melanie ‘Hye-jin’ Meyers

With over 22 years of experience in the food industry, Hye-jin started Tiny Chef as a way to explore Korean cooking through experimentation and merging different cuisines. Adopted by a white family in the Midwest, Hye-jin has slowly reconnected with her South Korean heritage through self-conducted recipes. In 2022, she reconnected with her birth mother or Eomma.

“I never worked at a Korean restaurant before, because I didn’t feel Korean enough.”

Growing up without traditional Korean home cooking, Hye-jin learned from books, YouTube videos, and good ol’ trial-and-error. Kimchi, a Korean staple, has varying tastes, as each family has honed recipes passed down from generations before. The first Kimchi Hye-jin made on the opening weekend of Tiny Chef sold out and has now become a signature staple for us. 

The rotating menu of weekly specials reflects Hye-jin’s constant learning. While customers’ favorites might make a return, also stay on the lookout for new specials. The Kimchi Cabonara recipe has been featured in the New York Times Cooking. From musabi burritos to udon with seaweed cream sauce, Hye-jin redefines what tradition is. Growing up without traditional tastes has given Hye-jin the freedom to explore.

“I’m not claiming tradition at all. I’m just being me, being influenced by Korean culture and learning about who I am.”
