Response 4: My Website is a Shifting House...

February 2, 2026

In the past, I mostly viewed sites as a shelf of content, resources, ideas. Sites were a vehicle to consolidate and communicate info quickly. I resonated with the perspective that a website can be more than just a container of content; its form can be the art. As an artist, the idea of a portfolio is daunting because of this internal pressure that my site has to be "perfect." Reframing it as a garden that grows over time and continously changes is liberating.

I also liked the descrption of websites as rocks thrown into the ocean. The emphemeral nature of sites in the rapidly chnaging web allows me the freedom to create more, iterate quickly without the worries of needing to make a "perfect" site or the burdens of a long-lasting site. One of my favorite "lost rocks" is the Every Noise At Once site, created by Glenn McDonald, a Spotify engineer. The site attempts to map every niche genre of music tagged at Spotify. Although this site is no longer updated since 2023, it's one of my favorites internet rabit holes I've stumbled across.
