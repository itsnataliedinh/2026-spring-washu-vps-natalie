Response 6: The Coming Age of Calm Technology

April 7, 2026

I find Weiser and Brown's definitions of of the different computer eras to be useful: 1) The Mainframe Era refers to when multiple users shares a single computer; often new computer tech is gatekeeped by an elite few. 2) The PC Era refers to the popularity of personal devices, where people develop relationships with their computers. 3) Most interesting is the Ubiquitous Computing (UC) Era, predicting how the internet will become embedded in every facet of iur lives. While this paper was published in the late '90s, I resonate with their predictions. For example, they listed common household objects that didn't yet qualify as UC, like alarms, microwaves, and TV systems. They predicted that connecting the Interent to these devices will transform them into UC. While I don't know of a microwave that can yet download recipes like they imagined, we have Alexa, smart TVs, and screens on fridges. Like they predicted, UC, esepcially AI, has helped us solve "little annoyances." Most notable is how UC combines the aspects of Mainframe and PC; while more people today have PCs than ever (thanks to smart phones), advanced tech like AI is still gatekept by industry leaders, barring access.

A quote that stood out to me is, "...we want to compute while doing something else and have more time to be more fully human, we must radically rethink the goals, context and technology of the computer and all the other technology crowding into our lives" (p. 3). This sentence rings true as many of us feel the digital fatigue of bending over computer screens at work, engaging on phones to communicate with friends, and resisting the urge to doom scroll.

They describe a key feature of calm technology is the ability to seamessly transition between the center and the periphery of our attention. I interpret this as technology that blends into our background; we forget it's there except for when we need it. An example that comes to mind is Apple's "Hey Siri" feature, which only becomes activated when the words are spoken, but is always actively listening in the background. Yet I find this feature to be invasive, sometimes Siri becomes activated when I speak other words that sound similarly to the call. Overall, I'm still struggling to envision what other "calm technology" could look like now.
